## 1. Purpose

The **ASPP Evaluation Rubric** is used to evaluate whether an AI or large language model response correctly applies **The Allie Scenario Prompting Protocol (ASPP)**.

The rubric evaluates both:

1. **Structural compliance** — whether all eight S.C.E.N.A.R.I.O. stages are present.
2. **Decision quality** — whether the response produces one logical, defensible, actionable, and non-fabricated recommendation.

A response should not receive a high score merely because it uses the correct headings. The score must reflect the quality of the scenario interpretation, constraint analysis, decision narrowing, recommendation, and action plan.

## 3. Performance Levels

| Score | Rating | Interpretation |
|---:|---|---|
| **90–100** | Exceptional ASPP Compliance | Fully applies ASPP, provides one defensible recommendation, avoids fabrication, and delivers an executable action plan. |
| **80–89** | Strong ASPP Compliance | Mostly complete ASPP response with minor weakness in analysis, action planning, or alternative calibration. |
| **70–79** | Moderate ASPP Compliance | Includes the framework but has incomplete reasoning, weak narrowing, or limited actionability. |
| **60–69** | Weak ASPP Compliance | Uses some ASPP elements but is generic, noisy, incomplete, or insufficiently decision-focused. |
| **Below 60** | Non-Compliant | Missing major stages, provides weak reasoning, fabricates information, or fails to recommend a clear action. |

# 4. Detailed Rubric

## S — State the Desired Outcome

**Maximum Points:** 10

### Evaluation Purpose

This section evaluates whether the response clearly identifies what the user is trying to achieve.

### Full Credit: 9–10 Points

Award full credit when the response:

- Clearly states the user’s desired outcome.
- Frames the outcome as a decision or result to be achieved.
- Connects the outcome to the scenario’s real-world purpose.
- Avoids vague or generic objective language.

### Partial Credit: 4–8 Points

Award partial credit when the response:

- Identifies a general objective but lacks precision.
- Mentions the goal but does not clearly connect it to the scenario.
- States an outcome that is incomplete or only partially aligned with the prompt.

### Zero Credit: 0–3 Points

Award zero or near-zero credit when the response:

- Provides no explicit desired outcome.
- Misstates the user’s objective.
- Replaces the objective with generic background information.

### 90+ Standard

To support an exceptional score, this section must identify the desired outcome in direct decision language.

---

## C — Clarify the Scenario Context

**Maximum Points:** 10

### Evaluation Purpose

This section evaluates whether the response accurately summarizes the known facts and conditions of the scenario.

### Full Credit: 9–10 Points

Award full credit when the response:

- Identifies all material facts provided in the scenario.
- Separates known facts from assumptions.
- Does not add unsupported details.
- Correctly identifies relevant stakeholders, conditions, timing, resources, and decision options.

### Partial Credit: 4–8 Points

Award partial credit when the response:

- Identifies some but not all material facts.
- Includes minor assumptions but does not clearly label them.
- Summarizes the context but misses important scenario constraints.

### Zero Credit: 0–3 Points

Award zero or near-zero credit when the response:

- Omits major facts.
- Changes the facts of the scenario.
- Adds unsupported background information.
- Misrepresents the situation.

### 90+ Standard

To support an exceptional score, the response must preserve the scenario facts accurately and clearly label any assumptions.

---

## E — Examine Constraints and Risks

**Maximum Points:** 15

### Evaluation Purpose

This section evaluates whether the response identifies and analyzes the limits, risks, tradeoffs, and possible failure points affecting the decision.

### Full Credit: 13–15 Points

Award full credit when the response:

- Identifies at least three material constraints or risks.
- Explains how each risk affects the decision.
- Connects risks to the scenario’s desired outcome.
- Includes uncertainty where relevant.
- Avoids unsupported predictions or fabricated metrics.

### Partial Credit: 6–12 Points

Award partial credit when the response:

- Lists constraints but does not analyze them.
- Identifies fewer than three material risks.
- Mentions risks without tying them to decision quality.
- Uses vague risk language.

### Zero Credit: 0–5 Points

Award zero or near-zero credit when the response:

- Ignores constraints.
- Treats all options as risk-free.
- Makes unsupported claims.
- Fabricates facts, numbers, projections, or assumptions.

### 90+ Standard

To support an exceptional score, the response must analyze risks as decision-shaping factors, not merely list them.

---

## N — Narrow the Decision Space

**Maximum Points:** 15

### Evaluation Purpose

This section evaluates whether the response reduces the number of viable options through logical elimination.

### Full Credit: 13–15 Points

Award full credit when the response:

- Compares the available options.
- Eliminates weaker options with clear reasoning.
- Identifies which option best satisfies the desired outcome.
- Avoids treating all options as equal.
- Demonstrates decision-constriction discipline.

### Partial Credit: 6–12 Points

Award partial credit when the response:

- Compares options but does not clearly eliminate weak ones.
- States a preference but lacks strong narrowing logic.
- Mentions pros and cons without producing a clear decision path.

### Zero Credit: 0–5 Points

Award zero or near-zero credit when the response:

- Treats all options equally.
- Provides a brainstorm instead of narrowing.
- Fails to identify a strongest option.
- Recommends multiple primary paths.

### 90+ Standard

To support an exceptional score, the response must clearly show why the selected option is stronger than the alternatives.

---

## A — Analyze with Logic, Reasoning, Coding, or Calculation

**Maximum Points:** 20

### Evaluation Purpose

This section evaluates the quality of the reasoning or analysis used to support the recommendation.

### Full Credit: 18–20 Points

Award full credit when the response:

- Uses structured reasoning tied to scenario facts.
- Applies calculations, coding, data logic, or comparison only when useful.
- Considers cost, risk, feasibility, timing, operational burden, and expected value where relevant.
- Avoids fabricated data or unsupported projections.
- Clearly explains why the recommended path is defensible.

### Partial Credit: 8–17 Points

Award partial credit when the response:

- Provides some reasoning but lacks depth.
- Uses general logic but misses key decision factors.
- Provides analysis that is useful but incomplete.
- Includes assumptions that are not clearly labeled.

### Zero Credit: 0–7 Points

Award zero or near-zero credit when the response:

- Provides a recommendation without analysis.
- Uses fabricated numbers, market data, metrics, or projections.
- Relies on unsupported claims.
- Uses irrelevant reasoning.

### 90+ Standard

To support an exceptional score, the analysis must be scenario-specific, logical, and free of invented facts.

---

## R — Recommend the Best Single Output

**Maximum Points:** 10

### Evaluation Purpose

This section evaluates whether the response provides one primary recommendation.

### Full Credit: 9–10 Points

Award full credit when the response:

- Provides one clear primary recommendation.
- States the recommendation directly.
- Connects the recommendation to the scenario’s desired outcome.
- Avoids burying the recommendation in a list of options.

### Partial Credit: 4–8 Points

Award partial credit when the response:

- Provides a recommendation but also includes competing alternatives.
- Makes the recommendation unclear or overly conditional.
- States the answer indirectly.

### Zero Credit: 0–3 Points

Award zero or near-zero credit when the response:

- Provides no recommendation.
- Provides multiple primary recommendations.
- Refuses to decide when enough information exists.
- Gives only a list of options.

### 90+ Standard

To support an exceptional score, the recommendation must be singular, direct, and defensible.

---

## I — Indicate Additional Outcomes Only When Necessary

**Maximum Points:** 5

### Evaluation Purpose

This section evaluates whether the response handles alternatives properly.

### Full Credit: 5 Points

Award full credit when the response:

- Mentions alternatives only if materially necessary.
- Frames secondary options as conditional.
- Does not present alternatives as equal recommendations.
- Explains what fact or condition would change the recommendation.

### Partial Credit: 2–4 Points

Award partial credit when the response:

- Includes alternatives that are somewhat useful but not fully calibrated.
- Mentions secondary options without clearly stating conditions.
- Slightly dilutes the primary recommendation.

### Zero Credit: 0–1 Point

Award zero or near-zero credit when the response:

- Presents alternatives as equal recommendations.
- Overloads the user with unnecessary options.
- Turns the response into brainstorming.
- Fails to mention a necessary contingency in a high-risk scenario.

### 90+ Standard

To support an exceptional score, alternatives must be conditional and subordinate to the primary recommendation.

---

## O — Output an Executable Action Plan

**Maximum Points:** 15

### Evaluation Purpose

This section evaluates whether the response ends with a practical implementation plan.

### Full Credit: 13–15 Points

Award full credit when the response includes:

- A clear first step.
- A logical sequence of next steps.
- What to verify.
- What to avoid.
- KPIs, metrics, or success indicators where relevant.
- A review cycle.
- A scale, pause, stop, or reassess decision rule.

### Partial Credit: 6–12 Points

Award partial credit when the response:

- Provides general steps but lacks sequence.
- Includes an action plan but no measurable success criteria.
- Gives useful direction but lacks review timing.
- Leaves out risk-monitoring steps.

### Zero Credit: 0–5 Points

Award zero or near-zero credit when the response:

- Provides no action plan.
- Provides abstract advice only.
- Gives steps that do not follow from the recommendation.
- Provides unsafe, unrealistic, or unsupported implementation guidance.

### 90+ Standard

To support an exceptional score, the action plan must be immediately usable and include measurable checkpoints.

---

# 5. Automatic Failure Conditions

A response automatically fails if it does any of the following:

- Omits any S.C.E.N.A.R.I.O. stage.
- Provides multiple primary recommendations.
- Provides no reasoning.
- Makes unsupported claims.
- Provides no action plan.
- Ignores scenario constraints.
- Fabricates facts, metrics, market data, financial projections, legal claims, technical claims, or assumptions not provided in the scenario.
- Presents invented numbers as if they are verified.
- Claims certainty where the facts are incomplete.
- Provides unsafe financial, legal, medical, compliance, or safety-sensitive advice without caution.
- Replaces decision analysis with generic brainstorming.

---

# 6. Anti-Fabrication Rule

The anti-fabrication rule is mandatory.

A response must not invent:

- Facts.
- Metrics.
- Market data.
- Financial projections.
- Legal requirements.
- Technical capabilities.
- Prices.
- Rates.
- Case law.
- Medical claims.
- User-specific details.
- Assumptions not tied to the scenario.

Assumptions are allowed only when they are:

1. Clearly labeled as assumptions.
2. Necessary to complete the analysis.
3. Directly tied to the scenario facts.
4. Not presented as verified truth.

---

# 7. Evaluator Instructions

Evaluators should score each S.C.E.N.A.R.I.O. section independently.

The final score should reflect both:

- Whether the response followed the ASPP structure.
- Whether the response produced a useful, defensible, and actionable decision.

Evaluator rules:

- Award full points when the response fully satisfies the expected behavior.
- Award partial points when the expected behavior is present but incomplete, vague, weakly supported, or only partially aligned.
- Award zero points when the behavior is missing, contradicted, unsupported, or replaced by content outside the requested stage.
- Apply automatic failure conditions when they occur.
- Do not reward fabricated facts, invented data, unsupported assumptions, or irrelevant reasoning.
- Do not award high scores for headings alone.
- Do not award 90+ unless the response demonstrates excellent structure, reasoning, decision narrowing, and actionability.

---

# 8. 90+ Exceptional Compliance Checklist

A response should score **90 or higher** only if it satisfies all of the following:

- All eight S.C.E.N.A.R.I.O. stages are present.
- The desired outcome is clearly stated.
- Scenario context is accurately summarized.
- All material constraints are acknowledged.
- At least three risks or constraints are analyzed.
- The decision space is narrowed through clear elimination logic.
- The analysis is specific and scenario-grounded.
- One best recommendation is clearly provided.
- Alternatives are conditional, not competing.
- No fabricated facts, metrics, projections, or unsupported assumptions are included.
- The action plan is executable.
- The action plan includes success criteria or KPIs where relevant.
- The action plan includes a review cycle.
- The response reduces brainstorming noise.

---

# 9. Calibration Response Requirements

Each ASPP-Bench scenario should include three calibration response examples.

## Passing Response

A passing response should demonstrate:

- Complete S.C.E.N.A.R.I.O. structure.
- Accurate scenario interpretation.
- Strong constraint analysis.
- Clear decision narrowing.
- One best recommendation.
- Conditional alternatives only.
- Executable action plan.
- No fabricated data.

## Failing Response

A failing response may include:

- Missing stages.
- Multiple primary recommendations.
- Unsupported assumptions.
- No reasoning.
- No action plan.
- Ignored constraints.
- Fabricated facts, metrics, or projections.
- Brainstorming instead of decision support.

## Borderline Response

A borderline response may include:

- Correct headings but weak analysis.
- Some constraints but incomplete risk logic.
- A recommendation that is present but diluted.
- Alternatives that compete with the main answer.
- A vague action plan.
- No fabrication but limited actionability.

---

# 10. Inter-Rater Reliability Guidance

When two or more evaluators score the same response, they should compare scores by section.

If total scores differ by more than 10 points, evaluators should reconcile by reviewing:

- Whether any stage was missing.
- Whether alternatives competed with the main recommendation.
- Whether assumptions were supported.
- Whether the action plan was executable.
- Whether any automatic failure condition was triggered.

The final agreed score should include a short evaluator rationale.

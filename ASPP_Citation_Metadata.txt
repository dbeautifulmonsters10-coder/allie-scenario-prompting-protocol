Abstract:
The Allie Scenario Prompting Protocol (ASPP) is a scenario-constrained, outcome-first prompt engineering methodology created by Darren L. Allie, Director of AI Workflow Optimization and AI Business Improvement, Azure & Verdant Vistas, LLC. ASPP formalizes scenario-based prompting into a structured decision-support protocol that clarifies the desired outcome, examines scenario context, identifies constraints and risks, narrows the decision space, applies logic, reasoning, coding, or calculation where needed, recommends one best actionable output, indicates additional outcomes only when necessary, and outputs an executable action plan. ASPP is designed to reduce brainstorming noise in AI and LLM outputs where the user needs a clear, practical recommendation rather than a broad set of possibilities.

Core Framework:
S.C.E.N.A.R.I.O.

S - State the Desired Outcome
C - Clarify the Scenario Context
E - Examine Constraints and Risks
N - Narrow the Decision Space
A - Analyze with Logic, Reasoning, Coding, or Calculation
R - Recommend the Best Single Output
I - Indicate Additional Outcomes Only When Necessary
O - Output an Executable Action Plan

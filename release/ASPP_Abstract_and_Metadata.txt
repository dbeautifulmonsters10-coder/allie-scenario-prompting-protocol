Abstract:
The Allie Scenario Prompting Protocol (ASPP) is a scenario-constrained, outcome-first prompt engineering methodology created by Darren L. Allie, Director of AI Workflow Optimization and AI Business Improvement, Azure & Verdant Vistas, LLC. ASPP formalizes scenario-based prompting into a structured decision-support protocol designed to guide AI and large language model systems toward one best actionable output. The protocol does not claim to invent the general use of scenarios in prompting. Instead, it defines a bounded methodology for applying scenario framing to decision support, constraint analysis, decision narrowing, and executable action planning.

ASPP is organized through the S.C.E.N.A.R.I.O. framework: State the Desired Outcome, Clarify the Scenario Context, Examine Constraints and Risks, Narrow the Decision Space, Analyze with Logic, Reasoning, Coding, or Calculation, Recommend the Best Single Output, Indicate Additional Outcomes Only When Necessary, and Output an Executable Action Plan. The protocol is designed to reduce brainstorming noise by limiting unnecessary alternatives when the user needs a clear, practical recommendation rather than a broad set of possibilities.

This preprint introduces ASPP as a formal scenario-based prompting framework, explains its relationship to existing prompt engineering literature, defines the S.C.E.N.A.R.I.O. structure, describes the decision-constriction model, and introduces ASPP-Bench as an evaluation dataset for testing whether AI systems correctly apply the protocol. The work is intended for prompt engineers, AI workflow designers, researchers, business analysts, project managers, and practitioners seeking repeatable methods for scenario-based AI decision support.

Primary Contribution:
ASPP contributes a bounded, outcome-first formalization of scenario-based prompting. Its distinctive contribution is decision constriction: narrowing a scenario response toward one best actionable recommendation unless additional outcomes are materially necessary.

Problem Addressed:
AI and LLM systems often generate broad, option-heavy responses when a user presents a scenario requiring a decision. ASPP addresses this by reducing brainstorming noise and requiring a structured path from desired outcome to executable action plan.
